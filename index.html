Our Goals

Goals of ISAFA

Accelerate SAF Integration

Support the transition to low-carbon aviation by driving the widespread adoption and blending of Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) in global air travel.

Build a Resilient SAF Ecosystem

Strengthen feedstock supply chains, production capacity, and infrastructure to enable a scalable and commercially viable SAF industry.

Strengthen Global Collaboration

Forge strategic partnerships with governments, industry stakeholders, and international alliances to align SAF deployment with global climate goals.

Advance Policy & Regulatory Frameworks

Advocate for progressive policies, incentives, and global alignment to create a supportive regulatory environment for SAF development and adoption.

Mobilize Investment & Green Financing

Enable public-private partnerships, carbon credit mechanisms, and sustainable finance initiatives to accelerate SAF commercialization and cost competitiveness.

Drive Innovation & Technology Development

Support R&D in feedstock diversification, production efficiency, and emerging SAF technologies to enhance sustainability and scalability.

About us

Leading India’s charge for low-carbon air travel

The India Sustainable Aviation Fuel Alliance (ISAFA) is a pioneering initiative dedicated to advancing the development, adoption, and scaling of Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) in India. As the aviation sector continues to expand, reducing carbon emissions is critical to ensuring sustainable growth. ISAFA serves as a collaborative platform that brings together government bodies, industry leaders, airlines, technology providers, researchers, and policymakers to accelerate SAF integration into India’s aviation ecosystem.

With India positioned as the third-largest aviation market globally, the alliance plays a big role in supporting the country’s transition to low-carbon aviation by fostering innovation, policy advocacy, and investment in SAF technologies. ISAFA aligns its efforts with India's climate goals, including its commitment to achieving Net Zero emissions by 2070.
